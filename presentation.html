THE COOK'S REFLECTION
"The most valuable finding was one we could not have anticipated from formative research alone."
User-centred design didn't just shape our features.
It told us we were designing for the wrong audience.

LIMITATIONS

  • Only 5 participants: not enough to prove the paradox is general
  • Order bias: YouTube went first, so it had a 20-year head start
  • No install step: the extension was already there, install friction never tested
  • Hidden value: "bread vs bento" implicitly ranks foods, and content

NEXT

  • Test the new audience: 30 teens (12–18) and 10 parents, in person
  • Real-world use: 3 weeks at home, daily diary + weekly Mirror reviews
  • Mobile companion: 66% watch on phone, the extension is desktop-only today
  • Measure how it feels: 28 adjective pairs (dull/exciting, etc.) via AttrakDiff
